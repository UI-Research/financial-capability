Financial Capabilities Feature

When families are able to manage their finances and weather financial shocks independently, they and their communities are better off. But a sizeable share of families are in financially precarious situations.

Almost one in four families have no non-retirement savings and more than half have less than $5,000 in savings (figure 1).

Nearly 4 in 10 low-income families have no savings. But it’s not just a low-income problem: 8.7 percent of high-income families have no savings. The relationship between unsecured debt and financial security is less obvious: no debt might indicate financial prosperity or no access to credit. Families with more debt are typically more advantaged than families without debt.

Families who experience financial shocks—such as job loss, a disability that limits work, or a large drop in income—are more likely to be evicted, miss housing and utility payments, and receive public benefits

Savings can help cushion the blow and protect families. Even a small amount of savings makes a difference (figure 7).
